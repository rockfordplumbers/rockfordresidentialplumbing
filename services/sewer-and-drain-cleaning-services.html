Sewer and Drain Cleaning Services: Keeping Your Plumbing System Clear

Common Causes of Sewer and Drain Clogs

Clogs in sewer lines and drains often result from grease buildup, food waste, soap scum, and hair accumulation. Foreign objects, such as wipes and hygiene products, can also obstruct pipes, restricting water flow. Tree roots are another major issue, as they can infiltrate underground sewer lines, causing severe blockages.

Signs You Need Sewer and Drain Cleaning

One of the first signs of a clogged drain is slow water drainage, which may worsen over time. Gurgling sounds from pipes or toilets indicate trapped air due to blockages. Foul odors coming from drains suggest decomposing debris, signaling the need for professional cleaning services.

DIY Drain Cleaning Methods

Homeowners can try simple solutions to clear minor clogs before seeking professional help. Pouring hot water down the drain can help dissolve grease and soap residue. A mixture of baking soda and vinegar can break down organic materials, while using a plunger or drain snake may dislodge stubborn clogs.

Professional Sewer and Drain Cleaning Techniques

For severe blockages, professionals use advanced tools to restore proper drainage. Hydro jetting involves high-pressure water streams that clear debris and buildup inside pipes. Video camera inspections help plumbers locate blockages and assess pipe conditions, ensuring precise cleaning without unnecessary damage.

Preventive Maintenance Tips

Routine maintenance minimizes the risk of clogs and sewer backups. Avoid pouring grease, coffee grounds, or food scraps down the drain, as they contribute to buildup. Installing drain covers can prevent hair and debris from entering pipes, while scheduling annual professional cleaning ensures a long-lasting plumbing system.
